SDG Goals

The goal of promoting mental health and well-being is to ensure that students are emotionally healthy, resilient, and capable of managing academic, social, and personal challenges. This initiative aims to create a supportive and inclusive environment where students feel safe to express their emotions, seek help without fear or stigma, and develop positive coping mechanisms.

By raising awareness about mental health issues such as stress, anxiety, depression, and burnout, the goal is to encourage early identification and timely support. It focuses on equipping students with life skills like emotional regulation, self-confidence, mindfulness, and stress management, which are essential for both academic success and personal growth.

This goal also emphasizes improving access to mental health resources, guidance, and peer support systems, ensuring that no student feels isolated or unheard. Ultimately, promoting mental health and well-being contributes to healthier individuals, better learning outcomes, reduced inequalities, and a more compassionate and productive educational community.
